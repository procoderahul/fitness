SHOULDER WORKOUT

SHOULDER IS A VERY IMPORTANT PART TO MAKE YOU LOOK AESTHETIC AND STRONG. IT IS ALSO A BODY PART WHICH SHOULD BE TRAINED AND DEVELOPED PROPERLY BECAUSE IT IS INVOLVED IN ALMOST EVERY DAY TO DAY ACTIVITIES THAT WE DO DAILY.
THE TOP AND THE MOST EFFECTIVE EXERCISES TO BUILD YOUR SHOULDERS ARE GIVEN BELOW.
THESE EXERCISES WILL HELP YOU BUILD YOUR SHOULDERS IF YOU DO IT IN ITS CORRECT FORM OF DOING.

ARNOLD DUMBELLPRESS

  • üéØ : ENTIRE SHOULDER
  • SETS: 2
  • REP RANGE: 10-12
  • REST BETWEEN EACH SET: 45-60 SECONDS

As the name suggests that this is exercise is a legendary exercise named after our legend Arnold Schwarzenegger. Start light until you master the form. Hold a dumbbell in each hand with your arm bent, as in the top of a biceps curl, so your palms are facing you. Now, instead of pushing straight up, spread your arms to each side laterally, then press your arms up and twist your hands so your palms face forwards. For full range of motion, finish by pushing your head forwards and reaching as high as you can so your biceps are close to your ears.

SHOULDER UPRIGHT ROW

  • üéØ : LATERAL (SIDE) DELTOID
  • SETS: 3-4
  • REP RANGE: 10-12
  • REST BETWEEN EACH SET: 45-60 SECONDS

The upright row is considered one of the best muscle builders for the shoulders. It is also potentially dangerous for the shoulders, requiring perfect form for the best results and to avoid injury.Breathe in and brace the abdominals. Keep your back straight, chest up, and eyes focused forward. Lift the barbell straight up (toward the chin) as you exhale. Lead with the elbows and keep the bar close to the body. Your arms should go no higher than parallel with the shoulders; slightly less is okay. Pause at the top of the lift. Lower the barbell as you inhale, returning it to the starting position.

REAR DELT MACHINE FLY

  • üéØ : POSTERIOR (REAR) DELTOID
  • SETS: 3-4
  • REP RANGE: 10-12
  • REST BETWEEN EACH SET: 45-60 SECONDS

This exercise is done on the machine specifically used to train the rear delts of your shoulders. Sit on the pad facing the rear delt machine and plant your feet firmly on the ground. Your chest should be pressed up against the chest pad and your back should be straight.Grab the inside horizontal handles with your palms facing down. Your arms should be slightly bent. Contract your rear deltoids to bring the handles back until they are roughly in line with your shoulders.Squeeze your rear delts hard in this position and slowly return to the starting position.
